## Documentation 

## What did you choose to track, and why?
I decided for my data collection was that I would make a mapping of how many times I picked up my phone everyday. I thought that instead of making this an analog thing where I wrote tally marks on a page, theres a system on our phones as to how long or how many times we picked up our phones. I thought this process would help show example of my natural habits and limits the creation of the action of mindfully marking everytime to show how many times I pick it up.


## What was it like to collect and visualise this data?
Everytime I picked up my phone was not necessarily counted but it was an over look of the day from when I picked up my phone. On the days I picked it up most there must have been reasons like checking the time or notifications that pop up on my phone.


## What did you notice that you wouldn't have otherwise?
I think with what I have realised from my reflections was that the days where I was not coming into uni or having a less productive day of going places. I noticed that I picked it up less but not because I was preoccupied so to speak, But the fact that I could actually sit on my phone more at home making me “pick” it up less often. 


## What choices did you make for your data collection? What does it emphasise? 
Instead of surrounding my “data” collection with just the data itself I wanted it to detect the nature of a human habits with technology and something always accessible to alot of people these days, especially around the discourse surrounding phone addiction and social media effecting our attention spans.


## What is left out?
I think it would be the timelines as to when I am picking up my phone couldve added more detail and context as to how I operate in my daily life. I think it would be interesting to see when and question how or why someone would pick up their phone at that time and what would have been going on. I think in comparison to my timetable compared to the data collection would also add more information as to how this would look visually on a chart.


## How does this exercise relate to data humanism and the *Dear Data* project?
Again I think this fits under the data of the mundane things we do in our daily lives that we could be more mindful about. What I think I could do next time is create an exercise where I then mindfully pick up my phone and compare this with the digital collected data I was recording for this exercise to see the contrast and maybe why mindfulness surrounding our daily technology should be implicated.
